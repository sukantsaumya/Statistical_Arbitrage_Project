1. Pairs Trading Overview

Pairs trading is a market-neutral mean-reversion strategy. It identifies two assets whose prices historically move together (they are cointegrated). When the price spread between them widens beyond its normal range, we sell the expensive asset and buy the cheap one, expecting the spread to revert to its mean.

  • Goal: Profit from convergence of temporarily diverging prices.
  • Advantage: Neutral to overall market direction.
  • Risk: Structural change â€” the relationship may break down.

Johansen Test

The Johansen Test (1988) generalizes cointegration analysis to multiple time series. It uses a Vector Error Correction Model (VECM) to find one or more cointegration relationships simultaneously.

  • Handles > 2 assets at once.
  • Computationally heavier but statistically more robust.
  • Best for sector baskets or ETF components.
